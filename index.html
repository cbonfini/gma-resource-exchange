Teach migration better, together.

The Resource Exchange is where faculty teaching with Global Migration 101 share readings, videos, case studies, activities, and assignments that work in their classrooms — openly licensed, reviewed by our editorial team, and organized by module.

Submit a resource Browse shared resources

How it works

  1. 1

    Sign in & tell us about you

    The submission form requires a Google sign-in, and asks who you are, your institution, and where you're based — so we can credit you properly.

  2. 2

    Describe your resource

    Pick the module it fits, the type of resource, add a link or upload a file, and tell us how you currently use it in your teaching.

  3. 3

    Confirm it's free to share

    You confirm the resource contains nothing copyrighted and may be shared under a Creative Commons license (CC BY-NC 4.0).

  4. 4

    Editorial review & publication

    Our team reviews each submission, adds a short annotation, and publishes accepted resources to the module collections on this site.

Open licensing, in plain words

Everything published here is shared under Creative Commons BY-NC 4.0: anyone may use and adapt it for non-commercial teaching, with attribution to the contributor. Please only submit material you created yourself or that is already openly licensed — not publisher PDFs, paywalled articles, or copied textbook content. Linking to publicly available material (a news article, a YouTube video) is always fine.

What makes a great submission

  1. ✓

    Classroom-tested

    You've used it with students and it worked — tell us what happened.

  2. ✓

    Clearly scoped

    It fits one module (or names the modules it spans) and a clear type: reading, video, case study, activity, assignment, or dataset.

  3. ✓

    Ready to reuse

    Another instructor could pick it up and run it next week without asking you questions.
