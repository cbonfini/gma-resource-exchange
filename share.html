How to upload a teaching resource

Sharing takes about five minutes. Here's exactly what to expect, from opening the form to seeing your resource published with your name on it.

Open the submission form

Before you start, have these ready

  1. 1

    A Google account

    The form requires a Google sign-in (any Google account works — it doesn't have to be Gmail). This protects the exchange from spam and lets you receive a copy of your submission.

  2. 2

    Your resource

    Either a link (article, video, website, shared document) or the files themselves — you can attach up to 5 files, up to 100 MB each.

  3. 3

    Two sentences about teaching

    Be ready to describe how you actually use this with students — it's the most valuable part of your submission for other instructors.

Filling out the form, step by step

  1. 1

    Open the form and sign in

    Click Open the submission form above. If you're not already signed in to Google, it will prompt you — sign in and you'll land on the form.

  2. 2

    Tell us who you are

    Your name, institution or organization, and the country where you're based. Published resources are credited exactly this way: "Shared by [you] · [institution] · [country]".

  3. 3

    Place it in the course

    Choose the module it fits best (or "General / multiple modules") and the resource type — reading, video, case study, activity, assignment, dataset, or slides.

  4. 4

    Title and describe it

    Give it a clear title and a brief description: what it is, roughly how long it takes, and what students get out of it.

  5. 5

    Attach the resource

    Paste a link, upload your files (up to 5), or both. If the resource lives online publicly — a news article, a YouTube video — a link is all we need.

  6. 6

    Share your teaching approach

    Describe how you currently use it: where in the term, as homework or in class, what discussion it sparks, what you'd do differently.

  7. 7

    Confirm it's free to share, then submit

    Check the copyright box confirming the resource contains nothing copyrighted by others and may be shared under Creative Commons BY-NC 4.0 with attribution to you. Click Submit — done!

What happens after you submit

  1. 1

    You get a receipt

    A copy of your responses arrives in your email right away.

  2. 2

    Editorial review

    Our team checks that the resource opens, fits the module, and is safely shareable. We may adjust the module or type so it files correctly.

  3. 3

    Publication

    Accepted resources appear on the Browse page under their module heading, with your attribution, your teaching note, and a short editor's annotation.

What we can and can't accept

Great: materials you created (slides, activities, assignments, case studies), links to publicly available articles and videos, openly licensed datasets and readings. Not shareable: publisher PDFs, paywalled articles, scanned textbook chapters, or anything copyrighted by someone else — link to where it lives instead. One form per resource, please — submit as many resources as you like!
